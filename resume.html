Resume & Career Resources

Everything a CSE fresher needs to go from "job hunting" to "offer accepted" — resume structure, LinkedIn presence, and a preparation roadmap.

Fresher Resume Guide: What Actually Gets Read

Recruiters at high-volume hiring drives spend seconds, not minutes, scanning a fresher resume before deciding whether to shortlist it. Structure matters more than design flourishes.

  • Header: Name, phone, professional email, LinkedIn URL, and GitHub/portfolio link — no photo needed for most Indian IT recruiters.
  • Summary (optional, 2 lines max): Only include if it adds information not obvious from the rest of the resume.
  • Education: Degree, institution, CGPA/percentage, and graduation year — list your most recent degree first.
  • Projects (most important section for freshers): 2-3 projects with a one-line problem statement, the tech stack used, and a measurable outcome or feature highlight.
  • Skills: Group by category — Languages, Frameworks, Tools, Databases — rather than one long unsorted list.
  • Internships/Experience: If any, describe impact using action verbs and, where possible, numbers.
  • Certifications & Achievements: Only include ones relevant to the role you're targeting.

CSE Resume Example: Project Description

Weak: "Made a website for food ordering using React."

Strong: "Built a full-stack food-ordering platform (React, Node.js, MongoDB) supporting real-time order tracking for 3 concurrent restaurant accounts; deployed on Render with CI via GitHub Actions."

LinkedIn Optimization for Freshers

  • Use a clear, professional headshot and a headline stating your target role, not just "Student at XYZ College."
  • Write an "About" section that mirrors your resume summary but in first person and slightly more conversational.
  • List the same projects as your resume, with links to live demos or GitHub repos where possible.
  • Post occasionally about what you're learning or building — recruiters do check activity, not just the profile.

Portfolio Tips

A simple portfolio site listing your top 3 projects with live links, a short bio, and a resume download link is often enough. Prioritize working demos over visual polish — a broken "impressive" design hurts more than a clean simple one that works.

IT Interview Preparation Roadmap

  1. 8-10 weeks out: Revise core CS subjects (DBMS, OS, OOP, Networking) and pick a primary programming language to go deep on.
  2. 6 weeks out: Start daily aptitude practice (30-40 minutes) alongside 1-2 coding problems a day.
  3. 4 weeks out: Begin company-specific preparation — review the exact test pattern and past interview experiences for your target companies.
  4. 2 weeks out: Do mock interviews (with peers or seniors), refine your "Tell me about yourself" answer, and finalize your resume.
  5. Final week: Light revision only — review your own project explanations and get sleep before test day rather than cramming new material.

How to Prepare for Product-Based Companies

Product-based companies (as opposed to service-based companies like the ones covered on this site) typically weigh data structures & algorithms and system design more heavily, even at the fresher level. If that's your target:

  • Practice DSA problems by pattern (two pointers, sliding window, recursion, graphs) rather than randomly.
  • Be ready to explain time and space complexity for every solution you write.
  • Understand basic system design concepts: load balancing, caching, and database indexing at a conceptual level.
  • Expect deeper follow-up questions on your resume projects — know your own code well enough to defend every design decision.

Skills Required for Software Engineers in 2026

Beyond core programming fundamentals, employers increasingly expect familiarity with cloud basics (AWS/Azure/GCP fundamentals), version control (Git), and at least conceptual awareness of AI-assisted development tools, since many modern engineering workflows now involve pairing with AI coding assistants. Strong fundamentals in data structures, databases, and clear communication remain the foundation everything else builds on.
